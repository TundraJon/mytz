Local Etiquette & Laws

Brazil — São Paulo

💳 Tipping

A 10% service charge (gorjeta) is commonly added to restaurant bills. Tipping beyond that is optional. Taxi and Uber drivers don't expect tips, but rounding up is appreciated.

👗 Dress Code

Casual attire is fine in most settings. Dress modestly for churches or formal venues. Beachwear stays at the beach.

⚠️ Safety

Stay alert in crowded areas and on public transport. Avoid displaying phones, cameras, or jewelry in public. Use Uber or licensed radio taxis. Stick to well-lit areas after dark.

🚫 Scams to Watch

Beware of distraction scams and phone snatching — keep phones in your pocket when walking. Use ATMs inside bank branches, not street machines. Avoid unofficial money changers.

🍺 Alcohol

Legal drinking age is 18. Alcohol is sold at supermarkets, bars, and restaurants. Public drinking is generally tolerated in social settings.

📸 Photography

Ask permission before photographing locals. Avoid photographing police stations, military installations, or sensitive government buildings.
